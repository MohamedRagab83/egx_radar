## 3. Component Importance Ranking

**Most damaging to remove → most critical to keep:**

### #1 — SmartRank (CRITICAL)
**Removing it: 0 trades. Complete signal collapse.**

The patch replaced SmartRank with a momentum proxy: `50 + adaptive_mom×2 + (vol_ratio−1)×8`, clamped to [0, 100]. This proxy produced ZERO qualifying signals for the entire year.

**What this reveals:** EGX accumulation setups are characterised by *low or negative recent momentum* and *near-average volume* — base-building behaviour, not breakout behaviour. The momentum proxy systematically scored these setups below the BT_MIN_SMARTRANK=45 threshold, filtering every single one out.

SmartRank's composite formula (`accumulation_quality×0.40 + structure_strength×0.25 + volume_quality×0.20 + trend_alignment×0.15`) captures a fundamentally different signal than momentum. It weighs accumulation quality at 40% — the dominant factor — and deliberately de-emphasises trend direction. This is the correct architecture for EGX base-formation entries. **Do not replace, simplify, or re-weight this formula without extensive ablation.**

### #2 — Accumulation Detection (ESSENTIAL)
**Removing it: 0% win rate. Worst Sharpe in the study (−32.49).**

Without `evaluate_accumulation_context`, the system still generates 2 trades, but every single one loses. The Sharpe of −32.49 (vs −5.85 baseline) confirms that accumulation context is not a cosmetic filter — it is the discriminant between trades that have a structural entry basis and those that are pure noise.

The accumulation gate enforces: `accumulation_detected=True`, `entry_ready=True`, `fake_move=False`. Any one of these being False should (and does) kill the trade. The No Accumulation patch forces all three to True, which lets through setups that are technically in the right price zone but without the volume/structure confirmation needed to enter safely.

### #3 — Regime Filter (MEANINGFUL, NOT CRITICAL)
**Removing it: +1 losing trade, −8.3% WR, −1.20 Sharpe.**

The regime filter correctly identifies and suppresses 1 marginal trade per year in this dataset. With only 4 trades taken when the filter is off (vs 3 baseline), its absolute contribution is small — but *directionally correct*. The extra trade generated by disabling the regime filter loses, confirming that the regime assessment is screening genuine noise.

In a higher-signal-density period or with more symbols, the regime filter's contribution would scale up proportionally. **Keep it.**

### #4 — Portfolio Guards (NON-BINDING)
**Removing it: ZERO change. Identical to baseline in every metric.**

`compute_portfolio_guard` and `_is_high_probability_trade` are completely non-binding for the 2024 dataset. Three possible explanations:

1. **Signal density too low:** With only 3 qualifying signals in the entire year, sector caps (e.g. max 2 open per sector) and ATR position limits never come close to triggering. There are never enough concurrent open positions for portfolio-level constraints to matter.

2. **Upstream filters are already doing the work:** SmartRank + Accumulation + Regime have already filtered out everything the quality gate would reject. The 10-sub-check `_is_high_probability_trade` gate may be testing for properties already guaranteed by the upstream passage.

3. **The quality gate threshold (5–6 of 10 sub-checks) may be set lower than the effective filtering band of the upstream chain.** That is, by the time a signal reaches the quality gate, it already passes 7–8 of 10 sub-checks automatically.

**Conclusion:** Guards are not harmful, but they are not contributing measurable value in the current configuration and signal density. They become relevant only if signal volume increases significantly.

## 4. The Raw System Paradox

When ALL filters are disabled simultaneously (Raw System), unexpected things happen:

- **Trades jump from 3 → 12** (+300%)
- **Win rate improves to 41.7%** (best of all configurations)
- **Profit factor rises to 0.52** (best of all configurations)
- **Expectancy is still negative: −1.02%**
- **Sharpe is still negative: −2.30**

**Interpretation:** The 9 additional trades let through by disabling all filters have a genuine win rate above 40% — they are not pure noise. But the ATR-based sizing model + execution friction (slippage assumption + fees) consumes all alpha before it reaches the bottom line. Even at 41.7% win rate, the system cannot overcome the cost structure.

This points to a structural problem that **no amount of filter tuning will solve**: the reward-to-risk ratio on individual trades is insufficient to overcome execution friction when win rate is below ~55%. The issue is not filter selection — it is trade sizing and R:R expectations relative to EGX transaction costs.

## 5. Critical Finding: Statistical Insignificance

**3 trades in a 250-day year is not a sample. It is a data point.**

None of the conclusions above carry statistical significance at conventional confidence levels. The standard error on a win rate calculated from 3 Bernoulli trials is ±29%. The true win rate of the baseline could be anywhere from 5% to 62% with 90% confidence. This means:

- Every metric in this table carries error bars larger than the metrics themselves
- The "component importance ranking" is directionally reasonable but not statistically validated
- A system generating 3 signals per year is **not suitable for live deployment** without an expansion of the signal universe

**What would be needed for valid estimates:**
- Minimum ~30 trades for basic significance testing
- A 6–10 year backtest period to capture multiple market cycles
- Expanding to the full EGX 30 or EGX 70 index (not just 27 selected symbols)

## 7. What the Ablation Tells You About System Architecture

The ablation confirms that EGX Radar's signal architecture is logically sound for the EGX accumulation thesis:

```
SmartRank (composite, accumulation-first)
    └── gates: does this setup have accumulation structure AND volume quality AND trend alignment?
         └── Accumulation Detection
              └── confirms: is the stock actually being accumulated now (vs. distributing)?
                   └── Regime Filter
                        └── asks: is the market environment safe to enter exposure?
                             └── Portfolio Guards (currently dormant)
                                  └── would ask: can we size this given open risk?
```

Each layer answers a different question. The first two layers (SmartRank + Accumulation) answer **"is this a real setup?"** — and they are indispensable. The third layer (Regime) answers **"is the timing right?"** — and it is modestly valuable. The fourth layer (Guards) answers **"can we afford this position?"** — and it is currently irrelevant because the upstream layers never allow enough simultaneous signals to stress it.

The architecture is correct. The problem is throughput: with 27 symbols, only 3 setups per year clear all filters. That is a discovery rate of ~0.4% (3/750 symbol-months). Either the filters are correctly identifying 3 genuine institutional accumulation setups per year in this symbol list — which would be very low but not impossible for a concentrated quality-first approach — or the filter parameters need calibration on a broader historical dataset.
